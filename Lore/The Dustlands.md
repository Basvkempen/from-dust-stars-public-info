*The eastern expanse of Eldoria — flat plains, rocky badlands, and honest work.*

---

## The Land

Flat plains give way to rocky badlands, which rise into the foothills of the Grayvein Mountains. It's not pretty country, but it's honest country.

**Duskmoor** is the heart of it all — a fog-wrapped city where three rivers meet. It's where the ore gets refined, the deals get made, and the powerful play their games behind closed doors. The Council of Veils runs things there, though everyone knows the True Hand Trade Company has more say than any elected body.

**Grimbarrow** sits in the mountain foothills west of Duskmoor, a mining town that feeds Duskmoor's forges. Hard people doing hard work. The pay is decent if you survive.

Between them and scattered across the plains are dozens of smaller settlements — farming villages, waystation towns, and places that exist because someone has to grow the food, raise the livestock, and keep the roads clear.

---

## The True Hand Trade Company

You can't talk about the Dustlands without talking about the True Hand. Their symbol — an open palm grasping a golden coin — is everywhere: on warehouses, on contracts, on the side of wagons rolling through town.

They started as an honest trading company generations ago, or so the story goes. Now they're something bigger. They run the trade routes. They fund the mines. They employ the guards. They own the **Iron Serpent**, the great train line that connects the Dustlands' settlements.

Most folks have a complicated relationship with the True Hand. They bring goods, create jobs, and keep commerce flowing. They also set the prices, write the contracts, and have a way of owning everything you need. Cross them and work dries up. Stay in their good graces and life goes on.

It's not evil, exactly. It's just business. That's what they say, anyway.

---

## What Everyone Knows

- The **Iron Serpent** runs on distant tracks — you can hear its whistle from smaller villages on quiet nights.
- Every settlement of any size has a True Hand agent, and every agent has more say than they let on.
- **Vraelos**, the Builder, is the Company's claimed patron — their founding charter invokes his name constantly. Old-timers mutter the Company's stopped understanding what he actually stands for.
- The Grayvein Mountains are where the real wealth comes from — iron, coal, and rarer things the Company doesn't talk about.
