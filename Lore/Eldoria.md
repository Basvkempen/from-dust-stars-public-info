*A guide for new arrivals and longtime residents alike.*

## The World Beyond

You live on **Lumara**, a world shaped by gods who once walked among mortals. That age ended long ago — the gods withdrew, leaving behind temples, prophecies, and the occasional miracle for those faithful enough to notice. Most people get by without divine intervention. They work, they trade, they raise families, and they try not to think too hard about what lies beyond the next hill.

The continent of **Eldoria** is bordered by towering mountains and deep forests, a land of stark contrasts — bustling cities where knowledge and power are revered, and quiet villages where the land's older magic is still felt in everyday life. It thrives as a centre of learning and trade, but its heart beats with echoes of a time long past.

---

## Western Eldoria

Old money and older traditions. The city of **Valoria** — the City of Knowledge — is built on scholarship, magic, and noble bloodlines that trace back to the Age of Gods. Scholars debate philosophy in marble halls. Temples gleam in the sun. If you have the right family name, doors open. If you don't, you learn your place.

---

## Eastern Eldoria — The Dustlands

Your home. Out here, nobody cares who your grandfather was. What matters is what you can do, what you can make, and what you can sell. The **Dustlands** earned their name honestly: the soil is thin, the wind is constant, and everything has a fine layer of grit on it. But there's opportunity here for anyone willing to work.

## The Grayvein Mountains

The great divide between West and East, running like a spine through the continent's centre. Dangerous passes, contested territory, and the source of the real wealth: iron, coal, and the rarer minerals that keep both sides of Eldoria running.

## The Gods

The gods don't walk the earth anymore, but their influence lingers. Most Eldorians aren't deeply religious — there's too much work to do — but everyone knows the names, makes the occasional prayer, and respects the traditions.

**The Primes** are distant and abstract: **Thessara**, the feminine divine of light and creation, and **Noctus**, the masculine divine of shadow and secrets. Scholars say they were once a single being called Aethon who split apart from loneliness. Common folk don't think about it much. The Primes don't answer prayers — they just *are*.

Below them are the gods who matter to daily life:

- **Solarian**, Lord of Radiance — popular in the west, temples golden and bright, priests who speak of truth and judgment.
- **Azelorn**, God of Shadows — quiet altars, prayed to for privacy and secrets kept.
- **Verdania**, the Lifegiver — prayers at planting and harvest, closest to ordinary life.
- **Thrennis**, the Stormcaller — respected and feared, thanked when the weather is good, appeased when it isn't.
- **Vraelos**, the Builder — big in the Dustlands, patron of craftspeople, smiths, and engineers.
- **Drakmoth**, the Bleeding King — the soldier's god, not of glory but of *cost*. Belongs to those who'd rather not fight at all.
- **Morveyn**, the Ferryman — tends to the dead. His priests perform the Naming, so he can record each soul properly.
- **Seraveth**, She Who Sees the Knot — deals in fate and prophecy. "Fate pulls but does not cut," her priests say.
- **Kalathos**, the Dreaming Deep — a sea god, little worshipped inland, but sailors swear by him.

## The Old Faith

Out in the wild places — old forests, forgotten hills, spots where the land feels *different* — there are older things than temples. The **Wild Wardens** tend to them: druids and rangers who keep ancient pacts with spirits that were here before any god was named.

Most people think the Wardens are relics of another age — eccentric hermits who talk to trees and complain about progress. But now and then, when crops fail for no reason or animals flee an area, someone goes looking for a Warden's help.

There aren't many of them left. The old shrines are going quiet, one by one. Whether that matters or not depends on who you ask.
