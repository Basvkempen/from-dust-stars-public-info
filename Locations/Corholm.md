A village of roughly 500 souls, three days' walk west of **Duskmoor** along the trade road that winds toward **Grimbarrow**. Corholm exists because of where it sits — a crossroads where the main trade road meets a smaller path leading south to the old quarry and the scattered homesteads beyond.

It's not much to look at. Nobody important lives here. No noble has visited in living memory. But there's honest work, and the rhythm of the place is yours.

---

## Layout

**The Village Centre**
- **Market Square** — the heart of things. A worn stone well at the centre, trade stalls every Tensday.
- **The Dusty Crossing** — the tavern. Two stories, timber and plaster, a fire that's never gone out. Everyone ends up here eventually.
- **The Smithy** — the only blacksmith for a day's travel.
- **The Sunshade Chapel** — a small stone temple serving Solarian, Verdania, and Drakmoth under one roof.
- **Veln's General Goods** — dry goods, tools, and the local True Hand outpost.

**Residential**
- **Stone Row** — the village's more prosperous families, in houses of stone rather than timber.
- **The Timber Quarter** — where most villagers live, simple wooden houses around shared wells.
- Scattered farmsteads within a mile of the centre.

**Surroundings**
- **The Grain Mill** — on Millrun Creek, north of the village.
- **Marta Vale's Bakery** — on the western road, up before dawn every day.
- **Cotter Dunwick's Barn** — on the eastern edge.
- **The Old Quarry** — two miles south, abandoned for twenty years.
- **The Greymarch** — rough scrubland to the west, where you go to hunt, forage, or disappear. Old ruins out there, and older stories.

**Roads**
- East to **Duskmoor** — three days' walk.
- West to **Grimbarrow** — three to four days' walk.

## What Everyone Knows

If you've lived in Corholm any length of time, you know:

- **The trade road** is the village's lifeline. When caravans stop coming, everyone worries.
- **Duskmoor** is where you go for opportunity, danger, or both. Some who leave come back successful. Others come back broken. Most don't come back at all.
- **The Iron Serpent** passes through on the distant tracks, maybe once a month — you can hear its whistle from the village on quiet nights.
- **The True Hand** isn't loved, but it's tolerated. The village agent is a fixture at the tavern.
- **Old Mirram's shrine** sits on a hill about an hour's walk from the village. People say she was a Wild Warden once. Strange, certainly — but she knows things about herbs and ailments the temple priest doesn't.

---

## Life Here

You know the rhythm of the seasons — the mud of spring, the dust of summer, the brief golden harvest, the long grey winters when the wind cuts through everything.

You know the faces at the tavern, the smell of bread from the bakery each morning, who's feuding with whom over a fence line or something that happened before you were born.

It's familiar. It's small. And it's yours.
